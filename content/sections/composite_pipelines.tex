\section{Composite Transformations}
\label{sec:composite}

The single pathways of Sections~\ref{sec:tok2tok}--\ref{sec:param-source} each characterize a single-step transformation of experience between one pair of carriers. Another class of method chains or closes several transformation steps into a pipeline, with experience flowing continuously among Narrative Tokenized, Schematic Tokenized, Policy parameters, and Evaluator parameters; the integration mechanism that joins adjacent steps is itself the method's main contribution, rather than any single-step transformation. A raw agent trajectory holds reusable strategy, locally effective actions, and noisy behavior all at once, and a single-step transformation struggles to handle these heterogeneous signals separately; a composite organizes them into a more reliable training, evaluation, or improvement signal through multi-stage processing.

This section identifies three composition patterns, each with an integration mechanism that acts on a different object. The integration of Evaluator--Policy Co-Evolution (Section~\ref{sec:coevolution}) acts on the coupling between the Evaluator and Policy parameters, which calibrate each other and co-evolve around newly produced experience. The integration of Refinement-Mediated Policy Internalization (Section~\ref{sec:refinement-mediated}) acts on the content of the experience, correcting, completing, or abstracting the raw trajectory before internalizing it into the Policy. The integration of Generative Experience Curation (Section~\ref{sec:experience-curation}) acts on the generation and distribution of the experience set, deciding which experiences are produced, which are kept, and how the overall distribution is composed, before using it to improve the Policy.

\subsection{Evaluator--Policy Co-Evolution}
\label{sec:coevolution}

Evaluator--Policy Co-Evolution refers to the Evaluator parameters and Policy parameters calibrating each other and co-evolving around agent experience. The abstract structure is a closed loop mediated by Tokenized experience: the Policy rolls out in the environment to produce new experience, and these trajectories on the one hand update or calibrate the Evaluator's evaluative capability and on the other flow back through the Evaluator's reward, critique, preference, or verification signal to update the Policy. Policy behavior drifts with training, and a static Evaluator goes stale---failing to recognize new failure modes and giving wrong evaluations of new strategies, while the Policy may overfit the Evaluator's bias; letting the Evaluator evolve with the Policy blocks this degradation.

The most common form of co-evolution has the Evaluator and Policy as two independent modules updated alternately. UI-Genie~\cite{Xia25e} is a complete instance: a GUI agent explores the environment, a reward model scores candidate actions and filters out high-value behavior to flow back as the policy's supervision, and failed trajectories are relabeled into learnable step-level signals through subsequent rollouts to refresh the reward model in turn; the two alternate over several rounds, so the reward model always updates to follow the agent's newly explored trajectories rather than going stale on new behavior. MagicGUI-RMS~\cite{Li26n} instead uses two reward models with a division of labor, one domain-specialized and one a frozen general model: the actions the former approves flow back as agent supervision, and the samples the two disagree on flow back as training data for the former. ECHO~\cite{Li26l} lists critic staleness as a core problem directly; its critic produces a natural-language diagnosis rather than a scalar score, the policy corrects itself by the diagnosis, and the two update in step, so the critic keeps following the policy's failure modes. RL Tango~\cite{Zha25y} alternately trains a generator and a verifier in mathematical reasoning, with the verifier learning only from the correctness of the final answer yet producing step-level verification feedback that shapes the generator, whose new solutions keep posing new discrimination challenges to the verifier.

RLAnything~\cite{Wan26u} extends this coupling from two parties to three---policy, reward model, and environment: the reward model updates with the policy trajectories and drives the policy in turn, and the failure modes it induces also tune task difficulty dynamically.

Self-Guide~\cite{Wan26aj} goes to the limit of the Evaluator and Policy sharing parameters: the same agent first generates a piece of verbal self-guidance at each step, a signal that both guides the action at inference and acts as an internal reward at training, so the closed loop degenerates into a self-rewarding loop on a single model.

\subsection{Refinement-Mediated Policy Internalization}
\label{sec:refinement-mediated}

Refinement-Mediated Policy Internalization first processes raw agent experience into a refined artifact of higher supervision quality, then internalizes it into the Policy parameters; the typical structure is Narrative Tokenized $\rightarrow$ Narrative/Schematic Tokenized $\rightarrow$ Policy parameters. The integration mechanism acts on the content of the experience---correcting, completing, or abstracting the trajectory, changing its semantics as a supervision signal. The premise is that a raw trajectory used as supervision directly still has room for improvement: a failed trajectory may contain a correct prefix and locally effective actions, and a successful trajectory may contain redundant actions or a non-transferable shortcut, so processing before writing into the parameters improves signal quality. Unlike the Narrative Abstraction of Section~\ref{sec:narrative-abstraction}, which serves retrieval, the artifact here enters parameter training.

\paragraph{Failure-Trajectory Rewriting.} This rewrites a failed or deviating trajectory into usable supervision, the most direct form being to locate the first error step and append a corrected continuation. Agent-R~\cite{Yua25c} distinguishes successful from failed trajectories in the actor's own MCTS search tree, locates the first error step of a failed trajectory, truncates its continuation, appends the correct branch and inserts a revision signal, then internalizes this correction into the parameters through SFT. STeCa~\cite{Wan25x} refines the granularity to a single step, using Monte Carlo continuation to locate the first action that deviates from the expert path and replacing the original segment with a reflection-bearing corrective action. LEAFE~\cite{Ge26} generates a natural-language summary at each rollback point on a rollback tree to guide the improvement branch, then has the policy reproduce the corrected action directly without reading the summary, through counterfactual distillation. CLEANER~\cite{Xu26j} targets the noise of tool-call trajectories, deciding adaptively after an error---by semantic similarity---whether to fix only the code or to deepen the fix into the reasoning as well, cleaning the trajectory into a clean version. AgentHER~\cite{Din26} rewrites not the action but the goal: keeping the trajectory unchanged, it reverse-generates a new goal that the trajectory's actual outcome satisfies, relabeling a failed run as a successful demonstration.

\paragraph{Posterior Reasoning Construction.} This appends a reasoning or reflection layer to a trajectory that lacks an explanation, giving an action-only execution record a trainable reasoning structure. ReAct Meets ActRe~\cite{Yan24m} has the agent explore to obtain an action sequence, then has a fixed ActRe module append the reasoning for each step in reverse, reconstructing an explanation-free action sequence into a trainable ReAct-style trajectory internalized through contrastive self-training. GUI-Reflection~\cite{Wu25b} fabricates errors offline and mines failures online, automatically constructing reflection-behavior data with verification, rollback, and retry, so the GUI model acquires reflection and correction.

\paragraph{Privileged Abstraction Distillation.} This abstracts a trajectory into a higher-level skill, knowledge, or hint, and distills it as training-time privileged information into a student policy that does not access it. Skill-SD~\cite{Wan26al} summarizes a multi-turn trajectory into a structured skill with success analysis, error analysis, and a golden workflow, injecting it only into the teacher, not the student, then distilling the teacher's skill-guided improvement distribution into a skill-free student through reverse KL. Online Experiential Learning~\cite{Ye26f} incrementally distills online trajectories into transferable experience and likewise compresses an experience-bearing teacher into an experience-free student through context distillation, showing through a controlled experiment that extract-then-internalize beats training on the raw trajectory directly. Memento No More~\cite{Ala25} uses a corrective hint as the intermediary: a reviewer identifies error patterns and gives a targeted hint, the teacher produces better behavior under the hint, and the student learns to reproduce it without receiving the hint.

\subsection{Generative Experience Curation}
\label{sec:experience-curation}

Generative Experience Curation takes the generation and distribution construction of experience as its core integration mechanism; the typical structure is Policy parameters $\rightarrow$ Tokenized $\rightarrow$ Policy parameters. The policy first externalizes a trajectory or synthetic task, and these Tokenized experiences are then verified, filtered, searched, or relabeled into training data close to the target distribution, which is then used to improve the Policy. The integration mechanism acts on the generation and distribution of the experience set rather than the experience content itself---deciding which experiences are produced, which are kept, and how the overall distribution is composed. The premise is that limited interaction experience does not suffice for reliable policy learning, so the experience source must be actively expanded and its quality controlled.

\paragraph{Exploration-Driven Synthesis.} This actively expands the experience source through exploration or synthesis. OS-Genesis~\cite{Sun24b} is a typical route: the agent does interactive traversal in a GUI environment to collect action trajectories, then reverse-synthesizes high-level tasks from the observed functionality, executes them to obtain full trajectories, and has a trajectory reward model score them by completion and coherence and sample them into the training set by score. AgentTrek~\cite{Xu24} swaps the task source for web tutorials, standardizing them into structured task specs that drive the agent to reproduce them on real pages and generate multimodal trajectories, then has a VLM check instruction-following and goal completion. AutoSurfer~\cite{Fai26} systematically traverses a website breadth-first to discover and compose tasks, then has the agent complete them into full trajectories on the real site. OpenWebVoyager~\cite{He24e} relies on no external task source: it first obtains an initial policy through imitation learning, then explores on self-generated tasks, with a frozen GPT-4o doing trajectory-level rejection sampling over rollouts and mixing only the trajectories judged successful into the next round of training. Bootstrapping Language-Guided Navigation~\cite{Wan24ad} has an instruction generator and a navigator promote each other, with the navigator filtering high-fidelity samples by SPL and nDTW to refresh the generator in turn, forming a self-refining flywheel. BLAZER~\cite{Das25} instead has a strong model zero-shot generate control programs in a simulator and keeps only the executable successful ones to fine-tune a smaller manipulation model.

\paragraph{Quality Filtering.} This verifies and filters the generated experience, deciding which experiences enter training and at what granularity. One work points out that a globally successful CUA trajectory still contains many locally suboptimal actions, and so scores each step 0--10, letting only high-scoring steps contribute supervision loss while low-scoring steps are kept only as context~\cite{He25g}. Language Feedback Model~\cite{Zho24e} first distills a large model's feedback on whether each action is desirable into a small feedback model, then uses it to filter the fragments of a rollout worth imitating. DigiRL~\cite{Bai24} does binary evaluation with Gemini on a real mobile device, then selects the most worthwhile tasks and actions through a task-level curriculum and a step-level doubly-robust filter. WebRL~\cite{Qi24} expands new tasks from a failure set, filters tasks by difficulty and feasibility first, and after rollout filters usable experience by an outcome reward model and actor confidence. PLD~\cite{Xia25h} and VARP~\cite{Sin25b} move the filtering forward into data construction: the former uses a residual actor to probe the base model's failure region and collects, through hybrid rollout, successful trajectories that contain recovery behavior and stay close to the deployment distribution; the latter projects 3D motion into a 2D sketch on the final frame, has a frozen VLM generate preference labels to train a reward model, and discards indistinguishable samples.

\paragraph{Search-Based Preference Construction.} This recomposes single-line trajectories into a denser set of preferences or paths through structured search. MCTS-EP~\cite{Xu25q} expands the embodied trajectory space with MCTS, both extracting successful paths for SFT and building preference pairs at branch nodes by long-horizon value for DPO. Agent Q~\cite{Put24} also builds on an MCTS search tree, combining node Q-values with AI critique to compile same-layer actions into node-level preferences for DPO. Trial and Error~\cite{Son24} uses no search tree, directly collecting failed trajectories and pairing them with expert successful trajectories into failure--success contrasts for DPO. Watch Every Step~\cite{Xio24} uses Monte Carlo continuation to estimate the return gap between an expert step and an agent-sampled alternative step, and from it builds step-level and trajectory-level preference pairs, jointly writing process-level local merit into the policy through step-DPO, outcome-DPO, and SFT.

\subsection{Discussion}

What sets a composite apart from a single pathway is that it treats the intermediate processing of experience---from raw log to learnable signal---as a design object in its own right. A single pathway either assumes this processing away and uses the raw trajectory as supervision directly, or hands it to a fixed mechanism; a composite breaks it open and makes it explicit, so a failed trajectory can be recycled as corrected supervision, a process-level signal can be supplied continually by an Evaluator in the loop, and the distribution of the training data can be constructed actively---none of which a single-step transformation can reach. The cost comes from the same source as the power: every intermediate stage made explicit can err, and the errors accumulate along the chain.

The form an error takes depends on where the pattern places the force of this processing: the coupling of Evaluator and Policy, the content of a single experience, or the distribution of the experience set. On the coupling side, if the Evaluator's training data comes only from the current Policy, the two slide together into a local optimum useless for external tasks. On the content side, once refinement misjudges the cause of a failure, or takes an accident for a rule, it contaminates the supervision signal itself. On the set side, the bias of the verifier and filter is amplified repeatedly by selection, and a flywheel without a diversity constraint converges to an ever-narrower band of experience. The three failures trace to the same place---the processing is handed to an intermediary (Evaluator, refiner, or verifier) that errs on its own, and its bias becomes the result's bias along the pipeline; such a bias is also hard to attribute in isolation, since the contribution of an intermediate stage can only be read indirectly from final performance and is entangled with the rest, so a composite's credibility depends heavily on the completeness of its ablations.

Whether correction can be applied to an intermediate stage depends on whether it can be inspected externally---a requirement that pushes nearly every composite toward a Tokenized relay, even when the source or terminal is Parametric: Tokenized experience can be read, scored, and rewritten directly by a verifier, reviewer, grader, or reflection, so quality control can intervene. Latent, mature as a single-step transformation (Section~\ref{sec:tok2latent}), barely enters the intermediate stage of a composite---it lacks the interface for an external module to insert evaluation or repair, and once written wrong it is hard to detect within the loop.

The design cost of the intermediate stage also explains the number distribution across the three classes. Refinement-Mediated Policy Internalization can start from an LLM's existing reflection capability, and Generative Experience Curation can mostly reuse an off-the-shelf reward model or verifier for filtering---both have a low barrier and the most instances; Evaluator--Policy Co-Evolution must specially design the synchronized update of Evaluator and Policy and also suppress co-adaptation, a high barrier with few instances. The boundaries of the patterns are not sealed either: RLAnything, beyond the Evaluator--Policy coupling, lets task difficulty adjust with failure modes, touching the construction of the set distribution at the same time; such cross-pattern composites, together with the item-by-item comparison between composite and single pathways, are left to Section~\ref{sec:synthesis}.
